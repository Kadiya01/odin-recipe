Pepper Soup

Description

A light, aromatic broth spiced with “pepper soup spice” (a blend of calabash nutmeg, uziza, and chili). It’s traditionally served hot, often with goat meat, fish, or chicken, and is prized for its warming and restorative qualities.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb goat meat (or chicken, fish)
  • 4 cups water or stock
  • 2 tsp pepper soup spice blend
  • 1 tsp dried uziza leaves (or substitute with a pinch of ground ginger)
  • 1 tsp ground crayfish (optional)
  • 1 tsp ground pepper (adjust to heat preference)
  • 1 tsp salt (to taste)
  • 1 tsp chopped fresh parsley or basil (for garnish)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped

Steps

  1. In a pot, combine the meat, water (or stock), onion, and garlic. Bring to a boil, then simmer until the meat is tender (≈30 min).
  2. Stir in the pepper soup spice blend, uziza leaves, ground crayfish, and ground pepper. Cook for another 5‑7 minutes.
  3. Season with salt, taste, and adjust the heat if needed.
  4. Garnish with fresh parsley or basil before serving.
  5. Serve hot, optionally with a side of boiled yam, plantain, or rice.
