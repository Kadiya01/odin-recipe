Egusi Soup

Description

A thick, hearty soup made from ground melon seeds (egusi), leafy greens, and assorted meats. It’s traditionally eaten with fufu, pounded yam, or rice.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups egusi (ground melon seeds)
  • 1/2 cup palm oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cups mixed leafy greens (spinach, bitterleaf, or pumpkin leaves)
  • 1 lb assorted meat (goat, beef, chicken) – cooked
  • 1/2 cup smoked fish, deboned (optional)
  • 1 cup stock or water
  • 1 tsp ground crayfish (optional)
  • 1 tsp ground pepper (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Heat palm oil in a pot over medium heat. Add onions and sauté until soft.
  2. Add egusi and stir continuously for 3‑4 minutes to toast lightly.
  3. Pour in the stock (or water) gradually, stirring to avoid lumps. Cook for 5 minutes.
  4. Add the cooked meat, smoked fish, crayfish, pepper, and salt. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Stir in the leafy greens and cook for another 5‑7 minutes until the greens are wilted and the soup thickens.
  6. Adjust seasoning if needed, then serve hot with fufu, pounded yam, or rice.
