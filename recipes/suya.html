Suya

Description

Suya is a popular West African street food—spicy skewered meat grilled over open flame. It’s typically served with sliced onions, tomatoes, and cucumbers.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb beef sirloin or chicken breast, thinly sliced into strips
  • 2 tbsp peanut (groundnut) powder
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper (adjust for heat)
  • 1 tsp ginger powder
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Wooden or metal skewers (soaked if wooden)
  • Fresh onion, tomato, and cucumber slices for serving

Steps

  1. In a bowl, combine peanut powder, paprika, cayenne, ginger, garlic, thyme, and salt.
  2. Add vegetable oil and mix to form a paste.
  3. Coat the meat strips with the spice paste, ensuring each piece is well covered. Marinate for at least 30 minutes (or overnight in the fridge).
  4. Thread the marinated meat onto skewers.
  5. Preheat a grill or grill pan over medium‑high heat. Grill the skewers, turning occasionally, for 8‑10 minutes until the meat is cooked through and slightly charred.
  6. Serve hot with sliced onions, tomatoes, and cucumbers. Optional: drizzle a little extra peanut powder on top.
